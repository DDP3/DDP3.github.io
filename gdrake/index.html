About Me

Websites are simply a necessity in the modern age. To be without one, even if the site itself is but a hollow, content-less shell, is to be without the distinct evolutionary advantage used to keep one's self afloat. Aha! As such, this very website serves as the thin, terra-cotta colored line between a world of pure reason, and the ever-howling, infinite depths of chaos. So much we ask of this piece of HTML, tempered with CSS.

But does it not provide?

That was the "about me" section that I originally wrote in this homepage, back when it was the review project. It represented a less informative but more poetic biographical style, focusing more on the core concept behind the style than the choices I made. I'll try to clear that up.

Originally, the site was almost entirely brown, and relied on thick, rounded borders to separate areas. It was a teetering, precarious mess, reliant on decimal percentages and margin/padding bargaining to push my messy divs into exactly where I wanted them. It was kind of a mess. The content for the new site essentially created itself according to the project's content requirements, but styling required an innovative challenge.

I designed it to look minimalist, warm, and calm, like the desert. Getting rid of the borders was necessary to creating this effect. I kept the browns, which hearkened back to tan sands and adobe houses. To separate the different sections, I used different colors while still sticking with the general dulled scheme. The site is designed to be horizontal on a desktop and pleasantly vertical on mobile. Instead of the default bootstrap navigation bar, I opted for simple text on a red background, complete with an indicator of what page you are currently on.

The challenge was re-learning how to style, without the continual fiddling of pixels and percentages in a css file. Where previously my page was messy behind and pristine in front, it became clean and flexible. Once I started modifying the content to fit the row formats better, it all clicked together. What I learned was that styling is not all done in post-production, but is an effort to be undertaken from the very beginning.
